National Beer Production and Consumption Trends
National Beer Production -- All Breweries

Over the last 10 years, US beer production has declined from 195MM bbls annually to 179MM bbls annually as consumer tastes have shifted, primarily to wines and spirits.

The large, national breweries, defined as producing 6MM or more barrels annually, have accounted for all of this decline, with production declining 35MM bbls during this period, losing market share to smaller craft breweries producing under 6MM bbls.

Craft Beer Production

Craft breweries have seen production grow by 19MM bbls during the same time period, with production increases fairly evenly split betwween larger craft breweries ((1-6MM bbls per year), which experienced production increases of 9MM bbls, and smaller craft breweries (less than 1MM bbls per annum), which experienced production increases of 10MM bbls.

While the grwoth in craft brewery production has tapered off, this segment is still seeing attractive growth of roughly 3.5% per year recently vs. declining total beer produciton of roughly 1.5% per year.
